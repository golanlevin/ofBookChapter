## Some Technical Workflows for CV in the Arts

"Computer vision" refers to a broad class of algorithms or workflows that allow computers to make intelligent (or even not-so-intelligent) assertions about digital images and video. Here are some basic tools of the trade: a vocabulary of ways in which images are processed to create meaningful experiences. 

#### Direct pixel mapping

- [Various mechanical mirrors by Daniel Rozin](http://www.smoothware.com/danny/)

#### Color tracking, bright-point tracking

- [*L.A.S.E.R. TAG* by Graffiti Research Lab](http://www.graffitiresearchlab.com/blog/projects/laser-tag/)
- [*IQ Font* by Zach Lieberman et al.](http://vimeo.com/5233789)

#### Motion detection (frame-differencing)

- [*Suicide Box* by BIT / Natalie Jeremijenko & Kate Rich](http://bureauit.org/sbox/)

#### Presence detection (background subtraction) 

- [*Standards and Double Standards* by Rafael Lozano-Hemmer](http://www.lozano-hemmer.com/standards_and_double_standards.php)
- [*Sorting Daemon* by David Rokeby](http://www.davidrokeby.com/sorting.html)

#### Contour tracing (brightness thresholding)

- [*Text Rain* by Camille Utterback & Romy Achituv](http://camilleutterback.com/projects/text-rain/)
- [*Nuage Vert* by HeHe / Helen Evans and Heiko Hansen](http://vimeo.com/17350218)
